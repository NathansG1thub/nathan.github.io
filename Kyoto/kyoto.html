About This Package

Once the imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, Kyoto is a city that seems to exist slightly outside of time. Ancient wooden temples rise from forested hillsides, stone lanterns line moss-covered paths, and geiko still move quietly through the narrow lanes of Gion at dusk. This 8-night package immerses you in all of it.

You will stay two nights in a traditional ryokan — sleeping on futons, bathing in a private onsen, and eating kaiseki dinners prepared fresh each evening. The remaining nights are in a contemporary Kyoto hotel, giving you the perfect balance of authenticity and comfort as you explore everything this extraordinary city has to offer.

Your Itinerary

  1. 1

    Arrival & Gion Evening Walk

    Arrive at Osaka Kansai International Airport and take the express train to Kyoto. Check in to your contemporary hotel and spend your first evening wandering the stone-paved lanes of Gion, Kyoto's historic geisha district.

  2. 2

    Fushimi Inari & Nishiki Market

    An early morning hike through the thousands of vermillion torii gates at Fushimi Inari Shrine before the crowds arrive. Afternoon spent grazing through Nishiki Market — Kyoto's famous covered food market stretching five blocks.

  3. 3

    Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

    Head west to Arashiyama. Walk through the iconic bamboo grove, visit the riverside Tenryu-ji Zen garden, and take the optional hike up to the monkey park for panoramic views over the city.

  4. 4

    Ryokan Check-In & Tea Ceremony

    Move to your traditional ryokan in the Higashiyama district. Afternoon guided tea ceremony with a local tea master. Evening multi-course kaiseki dinner served in your room.

  5. 5

    Philosopher's Path & Temples

    Walk the famous Philosopher's Path canal route, stopping at Nanzen-ji, Eikan-do, and Ginkaku-ji (the Silver Pavilion). A second evening at the ryokan with private onsen access.

  6. 6

    Day Trip to Nara

    A 45-minute JR train ride to Nara, where over a thousand wild deer roam freely through the park surrounding Todai-ji — home to Japan's largest bronze Buddha statue.

  7. 7

    Kinkaku-ji & Nijo Castle

    Morning at the Golden Pavilion (Kinkaku-ji). Afternoon exploring Nijo Castle, whose famous "nightingale floors" squeak with every step to alert guards of intruders.

  8. 8

    Free Morning & Departure

    A final free morning to revisit a favourite spot or pick up last-minute gifts at the Teramachi covered arcade. Transfer to the airport for your departure flight.
